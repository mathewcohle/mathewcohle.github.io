Things Tend To Change

January 8, 2020 2 minutes read

Recently, I read the book The Art of Living: Vipassana Meditation. There is chapter called The Two Rings1 describing story which goes something like this:

Two brothers inherit the property after death of father. Later on, they get into a fight and decide to split all the property equally – except packet with two rings, one expensive and the other just regular silver ring. The older brother argues his way to take the expensive one.

Both of them face ups and downs of life – the older brother with expensive ring gets excited a lot whenever something good is happening to him but suffers even more if anything goes wrong – eventually going mad and ending up taking electric shock treatments. Lot of good and bad happen to the younger brother except that he responds to all of it the same way, just accepting whatever life brings to him.

The difference is made by the words engraved on the ring which the younger brother uses to guide him through life: “This will also change”.

There is striking duality in that adage. Most of the people would agree with the part of handling negative experience peacefully. But what about not enjoying that brand new phone/promotion/your child winning school competition/<fill what fires your dopamine levels here>?

I suspect that implementing this for both situations is actually just precaution measure – based on the assumption we as humans are simply incapable of responding to positive signals while ignoring the negative ones. For now, though, I assume there is higher-order mechanism behind it.

Better to train whenever there is an opportunity.
