Doing No Quid Pro Quo

January 26, 2020 2 minutes read

If you are asked to do something, do it unconditionally – without expecting anything in return. No more scratching back only if you scratch mine.

Doing the favor conditionally builds an expectation which either ends with things going as expected or being disappointment if the promise is not fulfilled. Thus, it is equivalent of the trade with small upside but rather large downside. Lot of sadness around the world could disappear with better expectation management.

Moreover, doing things unconditionally might create positive environment as a side effect – at least people around me are often much more pessimistic than they should be given the reality we live in. Surprise of spontaneous altruism might put them off guard and eventually make them update their mental map of the world.

Note that applying the rule unconditionally to anyone would make you extremely exploitable. Apply it only after you make the decision to help or not – these two decisions should be treated independently.

If you find yourself on the other side of the deal, ask for favor without pretending you care about the person. Remember the situation when you replied to message from old friend you have not met for the couple of years, firstly exchanging few polite sentences before the real reason he is contacting you is revealed.

Whenever I find myself in such position, I always ask straight away for what I need and only after then I might start the conversation about how is it going – just if I really care.

The unfortunate corner case is the situation when you flip the wrong side of the coin. Imagine the scene from your favorite gangster movie – the guy making promise to be at service whenever he will be asked, without any questions, in exchange for the justice to his raped daughter. Here, the future favor serves as a currency with extremely unfavorable exchange rate. Make sure the trade is worth its price.
